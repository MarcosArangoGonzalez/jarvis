## Raw Extract (excerpt)

Coding is not enough🫸🏻

In 2026, it’s no longer enough to know how to code, train ML models and evaluate them, you need to have a profound understanding of the entire ML/AI product lifecycle.

That said, if you’re interviewing for the role in ML/AI you need to understand concepts like:

Data pipelines and data quality

Training serving skew

Batch inference versus real time inference

Latency, throughput and scalability

Model deployment and rollback

Monitoring for drift and failures

Tradeoffs between accuracy, cost and speed

I learned how to do this through a lot of trial-and-error, but if you follow YouTube channels like MLEpath, Marina Wyss or FreeCodeCamp, you can learn a lot

If you're serious about building ML/AI systems, follow @codingmermaid.ai
